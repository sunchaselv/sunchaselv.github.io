Frequently Asked Questions

Are you a second chance property?

No, we are NOT a second chance property and do not offer second chance rentals. If you have a previous eviction, your rental application will be denied.

Application requirements / application criteria?

Includes but is not limited to the following:

  • No evictions filed against you, ever—no matter how long ago. Even if the eviction was dismissed in court in your favor.
  • Income at least 3x the rent with legit paystubs to prove it.
  • No money owed to other apartments on your credit report (such as for breaking a lease). Even if you paid them off.
  • No sex offenders.

By applying, you agree to let us run a credit check and a background check on you. We also ask your previous landlords for references / rental verifications.

The only way we can tell you if your application would be approved or denied is by submitting an application and paying an application fee. But if any of the above points apply to you, then we recommend that you don't waste your money on the application fee.

Do you take Section 8 housing authority vouchers?

No, we do not take Section 8.

How much to move in?

Generally, we ask for the first month's rent + a security deposit, which starts at $200 and depends on your credit and application. We don't charge extra surprise "admin fees," "cleaning deposits," or stuff like that you're probably used to seeing at other apartment complexes.

Can I hold or reserve an apartment?

Yes, we ask for at least $200 (a "holding fee") that goes toward your initial move in costs (which is usually the first month's rent + the security deposit).

This $200 is not refundable if you back out. Once you pay it, we can wait until an agreed upon date for you to move in, but if you were to change your mind, we would not refund the $200 because we could have rented it to someone else while we were waiting for you.

Do I just pay electric? What utilities are included in the rent?

Yes, you just pay electric. Water, sewer, and trash is included in the rent. Unlike a lot of buildings, we do not surprise you with extra fees every month on top of the rent.

Electricity, cable, internet, Wi-Fi, or anything else is not included.

What if I move in the middle of the month? Is the first month's rent prorated?

To move in, you must pay the full rent. The days left over in the month will be credited to your next rent payment, so your next rent payment will be less (the pro-rated amount).

For example, let's say you move in on the 15th of June; there are 16 days left in the month. Before you move in, you pay the full rent, say, $800. Then next month, on July 1st, your rent is only 16 days worth of rent: $427.

We just want the bigger amount of rent first. In the end, you only ever pay rent for the days you were actually here.

If you are a current resident, see your lease for the full and only authortative policy on late fees. What is written here or on any other promotional material does not constitute a rental agreement.
